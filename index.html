UX Case Study · IXD262 Design Studio

Happy Space

An inclusive social platform empowering physically and sensory-disabled individuals to connect, learn, play, and build meaningful friendships.

Designer
Bhavya Sharma
Type
Mobile App
Platform
iOS & Android
Year
2024
01

Overview

The challenge we set out to solve

People with physical and sensory disabilities face significant social isolation. Mainstream platforms were not built with their needs in mind — leaving an entire community without a space to truly connect, grow, and thrive together.

01

Objective

Create an inclusive global platform where disabled individuals can connect, learn, play, and build meaningful friendships — fully accessible from day one.

02

Problem Statement

People with disabilities find it difficult to connect with peers in their community. Existing platforms lack the accessibility features and cultural safety they need.

03

Target Users

Physically disabled individuals (quadriplegic, amputee) and sensory-disabled people (deaf, blind, mute) who wish to connect with their community online.

04

My Role

Solo UX Designer — conducting primary & secondary research, creating personas, wireframes (lo-fi & hi-fi), affinity mapping, and usability testing.

02

Research

Listening before designing

Primary research involved direct one-on-one interviews and manual survey forms with users. Secondary research mapped the competitive landscape and identified market opportunities.

Primary Research

🗣️
One-on-One Interviews

Conducted in-depth conversations with physically and sensory disabled individuals to understand daily challenges, communication preferences, and unmet digital needs.

📋
Manual Survey Forms

Distributed paper-based survey forms capturing quantitative data on social media usage, community connection difficulties, and accessibility requirements.

🔬
Insight Synthesis

Empathy mapping with two distinct user archetypes — a community creator and a wheelchair-using college student — revealed contrasting but complementary needs.

Secondary Research

🏆
Competitor Analysis

Disaboom (direct) — social network for disabled people, founded 2007 but activity declined. Alvie — bridges isolation for people with disabilities. Dateability (indirect) — dating app for disabled users launched 2021.

📊
Market Breakdown

Mobility impairment holds the largest market share (35–40%), followed by hearing (25–30%), vision (15–20%), and speech impairment (10–15%). Each segment has distinct assistive technology needs.

🏛️
Saksham Disability Resource

Visited Saksham Resource Centre, dedicated to empowering students with visual impairments through academic excellence — provided real-world context for design decisions.

05

Design Process

From insights to interface

🗺️

Empathy Mapping

Built empathy maps for two key user types: a community creator advocating for disabled individuals, and a wheelchair-using college student navigating inaccessible digital and physical spaces. Revealed emotional pain points beyond task friction.

🧭

Current State Journey Map

Mapped the user's existing experience from social isolation (exposition) through failed connection attempts (rising action) to the climax of frustration — and the desired resolution of finding a meaningful community platform.

🧩

Brainstorming & Affinity Mapping

Generated 25+ ideas across sticky-note brainstorming sessions. Ideas were grouped into four core pillars: Community & Social Features, Motivation & Entertainment, Education & Skills, and Medicine & Health Support.

🏗️

Information Architecture

Structured a hierarchical IA from Splash Screen → Onboarding → Main Screen, branching into four main sections. Each section was further broken into accessible sub-features prioritized by user need frequency.

✏️

Lo-Fi Wireframes

Created grayscale wireframes for key screens: splash, sign-up flow, accessibility preference selector, home feed, community tab, and notes section. Focused on layout and content hierarchy without visual distraction.

🎨

Hi-Fi Prototypes

Elevated designs with a warm, pastel-forward visual language. Key screens included an accessibility onboarding flow (one-hand layout, voice control, large buttons), a personalized home feed, and a structured notes system — all matching the "Happy Space" brand ethos.

07

Core Features

Designed for every ability

Each feature was validated against user research — every interaction mode, every button size, every AI feature was chosen because real users said they needed it.

🎙️

Voice Control & Speech-to-Text

Hands-free navigation and AI-powered speech-to-text for users with motor disabilities like quadriplegia or amputees who cannot use standard touch input.

👁️

Screen Reader & Alt Text AI

Full screen reader compatibility with AI-generated alt text for all images and videos, ensuring visually impaired users have equal access to all content.

🤟

Sign Language & Caption Support

AI-accurate captioning for video content and dedicated tools for creating and sharing sign language videos — built for and with the Deaf community.

📐

Accessible UI Customisation

Users choose one-handed layout, large button mode, or voice-only navigation during onboarding. High contrast mode and adjustable text size available at all times.

🫂

Friend Finder & Social Circles

Connect with people who share similar disabilities and experiences. Gamified challenges and community events encourage meaningful interaction beyond passive scrolling.

🚨

Quick Help & Health Support

Emergency SOS button, telemedicine consultations, guided mobility exercises, medication reminders, and mental health chat therapy — healthcare access at your fingertips.

09

Usability Testing

Testing revealed strengths and gaps

Usability testing assessed the hi-fi prototypes across four key dimensions: interface clarity, navigation flow, accessibility features, and emotional appeal.

✅ What worked well

  • Clean, organized layout with large, well-spaced buttons suitable for motor accessibility
  • Logical navigation flow from splash screen through to content
  • Cheerful, cartoon-style design creates emotional uplift for users
  • Visual hierarchy is clearly present across all screens (titles, subheadings)
  • Tab navigation provides clear access to Community, Entertainment, and more

⚠️ Areas for improvement

  • Text-to-speech and voice navigation not visually confirmed in designs
  • Some text elements may lack sufficient color contrast for low vision users
  • Missing visual cues for haptic feedback and voice prompts for visually impaired
  • High contrast mode and dynamic font scaling should be added
  • Need more community interaction elements like "Join a Friend Group" prompts
  • User personalization features (avatars, named voice assistants) should be included

Every voice, every talent, every smile matters.

Happy Space is designed with the belief that disability is not an inability — and that with the right digital environment, every individual can connect, grow, and thrive.
